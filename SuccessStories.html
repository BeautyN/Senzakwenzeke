Success stories



SACBC Funding

Through the SACBC funding the Organisation managed to employ 23 previously unemployed youth of the community of Vosloorus, all the staff members of the organisation are youth fresh from high school most of them do not have working experience .all staff members were capacitated with community work skills, and trained on different community development skills

Peer Educators

Two young volunteers were trained as HIV and TB peer educators on 28 to 30 November 2016 they trained on basic HIV and TB .they trained on how to provide HIV/Aids and TB awareness to community members and facilitate HIV support groups they trained other care workers on HIV and transferred the skills they got from the training to them as the result of the training a HIV support group was established in the organisation for both young and adults infected and affected by HIV and Aids children support group consist of 15 male and females between 10 to 15 years of age adult support group consist of 20 male and females between 18 to 75 years of age .

Gender Based Violence

There are 4 Caregivers who trained as gender based ambassadors in the Organisation Were equipped with the skills to provide awareness on gender based violence to the community, and skills to facilitate support groups for victims of abuse. How to communicate with children about abuse and the importance of engaging with other stakeholders, referring victims of abuse to the relevant service providers such as POWA, FAMSA, Police department, Social Workers etc.

Families And Community Members

The organisation managed to recruit 160 community members of Vosloorus to partake on the family Matters program in 2016.Family Matters programme is a programme established to equip parents and guardians of teenagers with the skills to communicate better with their teenage children as means to reduce the number of children who get involved in sexual activities, drug abuse and crime due to peer pressure. All the participants graduated at the end of the training and they we presented with certificates this was a great experience and honour for all the participants as many never got the opportunity to graduate in their life.

Building Capacity Of Local Organisation

Senzakwenzeke Child and Youth Care Organisation is a new Organisation established by a group of young people in the community of Vosloorus who do not have experience in running the organisation. The funding project manager Mrs Nandi Sithole (SACBC ) assisted the organisation with developing policies , Standard Operating Procedures (SOP’s ) and guidelines for the organisation the organisation is now registered and operate using the standard policies it is able to manage the organisation funds and understand the importance of proper accountability .

HCT Campaigns

Through the SACBC funding, Senzakwenzeke Child and Youth Care Organisation in collaboration with shout it now, Aurum institute and other stakeholders have provided Awareness campaigns on HIV/ AIDS and testing in the community of Vosloorus to educate people in the community about HIV/AIDS to help reduce the fear of the unknown to the people who are reluctant to test due to stories related to HIV, Stigma and discrimination and deaths due to AIDS. 4 Campaigns were conducted in 2016, total of people tested in the four campaigns is 635. 419 children, 102 adults and 114 community members.

FSI Training

17 members were trained for the FSI (family support and interventions) including the project coordinator, M&E person and Caregivers. We have gained more knowledge on the following:

∗Support group facilitation
∗Emotional intelligence
∗How to encourage clients to disclose their HIV statuses to their children
